Think and Grow Rich

10 Core Principles by Napoleon Hill

1
Definite Major Purpose - The Power of Desire
The foundation of all achievement begins with a burning desire for a specific goal that becomes your definite major purpose in life. This is not a mere wish or hope, but an obsessive, passionate desire that consumes your thoughts and drives your actions every single day. When you have a definite major purpose, your subconscious mind works continuously to find ways to achieve it, and you begin to notice opportunities that were always there but previously invisible to you. This principle teaches that desire is the starting point of all achievement and that the intensity of your desire determines the speed and certainty of your success. The most successful people in history all possessed this burning desire that enabled them to persist through failures, overcome obstacles, and maintain unwavering focus on their ultimate objective despite temporary setbacks or discouragement from others.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Write down your definite major purpose in specific, measurable terms and read it aloud twice daily - once upon waking and once before sleep.
  • Create a visual representation of your goal (vision board, detailed description, or drawing) and spend 10 minutes daily visualizing yourself having already achieved it.
  • List all the benefits you will receive from achieving your goal and review this list whenever your motivation begins to wane or when facing obstacles.
  • Set a specific deadline for achieving your major purpose and break it down into smaller monthly, weekly, and daily targets that you can track and measure consistently.
2
Faith - Visualization and Belief in Achievement
Faith is the eternal elixir that gives life, power, and action to the impulse of thought, transforming mere wishes into their physical equivalent through the process of visualization and unwavering belief. This principle goes beyond religious faith to encompass absolute confidence in your ability to achieve your goals, even when circumstances suggest otherwise. When you develop unshakeable faith in your definite major purpose, you tap into infinite intelligence and attract the people, resources, and opportunities necessary for success. Faith acts as a state of mind that can be developed through repeated affirmations and visualization, programming your subconscious mind to accept your goals as already achieved. The most powerful aspect of faith is that it enables you to see opportunities where others see obstacles, persist when others quit, and maintain optimism during the darkest hours of your journey toward success.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Practice daily affirmations stating your goals as if they have already been achieved, using present tense and emotionally charged language that reinforces your faith.
  • Spend 15 minutes each morning visualizing yourself living your ideal life in vivid detail, engaging all your senses to make the experience as real as possible.
  • Collect and study success stories of people who achieved similar goals to yours, using their examples to strengthen your belief in your own potential for success.
  • Write down and regularly review evidence of your past successes and progress toward your current goals to reinforce your confidence and maintain unwavering faith.
3
Auto-Suggestion - The Medium for Influencing the Subconscious Mind
Auto-suggestion is the agency of communication between the conscious and subconscious minds, serving as the medium through which you can voluntarily feed your subconscious mind with thoughts of a creative nature or permit thoughts of a destructive nature to find their way into this fertile garden. This principle teaches that you have complete control over what reaches your subconscious mind through the thoughts you think and the self-talk you engage in throughout each day. When you consistently repeat positive affirmations about your goals with emotion and belief, you program your subconscious mind to work toward making those statements reality. The power of auto-suggestion lies in its ability to transform your mental attitude from negative to positive, reprogram limiting beliefs, and create new patterns of thinking that support your success. By mastering this principle, you become the architect of your own thoughts and the director of your subconscious mind's tremendous power.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Create and recite personalized affirmations related to your goals with strong emotion and conviction at least three times daily, feeling the truth of each statement.
  • Practice positive self-talk throughout the day by consciously replacing negative thoughts with constructive ones that support your goals and build confidence.
  • Record yourself reading your goals and affirmations, then listen to this recording before sleep to program your subconscious mind during its most receptive state.
  • Monitor your internal dialogue and immediately correct any self-limiting statements by reframing them in positive terms that align with your desired outcomes.
4
Specialized Knowledge - Personal Experiences or Observations
General knowledge, regardless of its quantity or variety, is of little use in the accumulation of wealth, while specialized knowledge focused on a specific field or goal is invaluable and essential for success. This principle emphasizes that success comes not from knowing everything about everything, but from becoming exceptionally knowledgeable and skilled in the specific area related to your definite major purpose. Specialized knowledge must be practical and immediately applicable to your goals, and it can be acquired through formal education, personal experience, or by surrounding yourself with experts who possess the knowledge you need. The most successful individuals understand that knowledge is only potential power and becomes actual power only when it is organized into definite plans of action and directed toward a specific end. This principle also teaches that you don't need to personally possess all the specialized knowledge required for success, but you must know how to access and organize such knowledge when needed.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Identify the specific knowledge and skills required for your goal and create a learning plan with daily study sessions focused on acquiring this expertise.
  • Find and connect with experts in your field of interest, building relationships with people who possess the specialized knowledge you need for success.
  • Apply new knowledge immediately by taking action on what you learn, transforming information into practical experience through consistent implementation.
  • Dedicate at least one hour daily to studying materials directly related to your major purpose, building your expertise systematically over time.
5
Imagination - The Workshop of the Mind
The imagination is the workshop of the mind where all plans are created and where the impulse of desire is given shape, form, and action through the creative faculties of your mind. This principle distinguishes between synthetic imagination, which creates new combinations from existing concepts, and creative imagination, which provides direct communication with infinite intelligence for entirely original ideas. Imagination is the faculty through which you can see your goals as already accomplished, allowing you to create detailed mental blueprints of your desired future before taking physical action to make it reality. When properly developed and directed, imagination becomes your most valuable tool for problem-solving, opportunity recognition, and innovative thinking that sets you apart from the competition. The most successful people have learned to harness their imagination to visualize solutions, anticipate challenges, and create detailed plans for achieving their objectives with remarkable clarity and precision.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Practice creative visualization by spending 20 minutes daily imagining your ideal future in vivid detail, using all your senses to make the experience realistic.
  • Use your imagination to solve current challenges by visualizing multiple solutions and mentally testing each one before taking action in the real world.
  • Create detailed mental movies of your goals being achieved, including the steps required, potential obstacles, and your successful strategies for overcoming them.
  • Engage in regular brainstorming sessions where you use your imagination to generate creative ideas for improving your situation or reaching your objectives.
6
Organized Planning - The Crystallization of Desire into Action
The transformation of desire into its monetary equivalent requires organized planning that bridges the gap between where you are and where you want to be through systematic, coordinated effort. This principle teaches that desires without definite plans remain wishes, while desires backed by organized planning become goals with real potential for achievement. Effective planning requires careful analysis of your objective, identification of required resources, development of timelines, and creation of contingency strategies for overcoming anticipated obstacles. The most successful individuals understand that plans must be flexible enough to adapt to changing circumstances while remaining focused on the ultimate objective, and they are willing to modify their methods while never compromising their purpose. Organized planning also involves the formation of alliances with others whose cooperation, knowledge, or resources are necessary for the successful execution of your plans, creating a mastermind alliance that multiplies your individual capabilities.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Create a detailed written plan for achieving your major goal, including specific steps, deadlines, required resources, and measurable milestones.
  • Review and adjust your plans weekly, evaluating progress, identifying obstacles, and making necessary modifications while maintaining focus on your ultimate objective.
  • Break down large goals into smaller, manageable daily actions that you can consistently execute, building momentum through systematic progress.
  • Develop contingency plans for potential challenges or setbacks, preparing alternative strategies that allow you to adapt quickly without losing momentum.
7
Decision - The Mastery of Procrastination
Successful people have the habit of reaching decisions promptly and changing them slowly if at all, while unsuccessful people reach decisions slowly and change them frequently and quickly, demonstrating lack of definiteness of purpose. This principle reveals that procrastination is the opposite of decision and is a common enemy that must be conquered by anyone who seeks success in any endeavor. The ability to make quick decisions comes from knowing exactly what you want and having confidence in your judgment, while indecision creates doubt, fear, and missed opportunities that can derail your progress toward success. Decision-making power is developed through practice and becomes stronger with each choice you make, building a momentum of confidence that attracts success and repels failure. The most successful individuals understand that making no decision is actually a decision to let circumstances control their destiny, so they take control by making definite decisions and following them with persistent action.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Practice making small decisions quickly throughout the day to build your decision-making muscle and reduce the habit of procrastination.
  • Set deadlines for making important decisions and stick to them, avoiding the tendency to gather endless information as an excuse for delay.
  • Write down your decisions and the reasons behind them to increase commitment and accountability, making it harder to reverse course unnecessarily.
  • Limit the number of people whose opinions you seek when making decisions, choosing only trusted advisors who support your success and understand your goals.
8
Persistence - The Sustained Effort Necessary to Induce Faith
Persistence is an essential factor in the procedure of transmuting desire into its monetary equivalent, serving as the foundation upon which all achievement is built through sustained effort and unwavering determination. This principle teaches that most people give up when they encounter the first signs of opposition or temporary defeat, while successful individuals understand that these obstacles are simply tests of their desire and commitment to their goals. Persistence is directly related to the definiteness of purpose, and when you have a clear, burning desire for a specific objective, you naturally develop the persistence required to overcome any obstacle that stands in your way. The development of persistence begins with these four simple steps: a definite purpose backed by burning desire, a definite plan expressed in continuous action, a mind closed tightly against all negative and discouraging influences, and a friendly alliance with those who encourage you to follow through with your plan. Persistence is a state of mind that can be cultivated through practice and becomes a habit that virtually guarantees success in any worthy endeavor.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Commit to taking at least one action toward your goal every single day, regardless of how small, to build the habit of persistent effort.
  • When facing setbacks or obstacles, immediately look for lessons learned and alternative approaches rather than considering giving up on your objective.
  • Surround yourself with persistent, success-minded people who will encourage your efforts and hold you accountable for following through on your commitments.
  • Keep a success journal documenting your daily progress and victories, no matter how small, to maintain motivation during challenging periods.
9
Power of the Master Mind - The Driving Force
The Master Mind is defined as coordination of knowledge and effort between two or more people who work toward a definite purpose in a spirit of harmony, creating a power that far exceeds the sum of individual capabilities. This principle reveals that no individual has sufficient experience, education, native ability, and knowledge to ensure the accumulation of great wealth without the cooperation of others who supply the deficiencies. When minds come together in harmony, they create a third mind that has access to knowledge and insights not available to any individual member of the group, tapping into what Hill calls "infinite intelligence." The Master Mind alliance provides two distinct advantages: economic advantage through the cooperation of others in your efforts, and psychic advantage through the harmony of purpose that creates positive mental energy and creative power. The most successful people in history have all employed the Master Mind principle, surrounding themselves with advisors, partners, and supporters whose combined knowledge and effort made their achievements possible.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Identify and recruit 3-5 people who share your vision and can contribute different skills, knowledge, or resources to help achieve your major purpose.
  • Schedule regular meetings with your Master Mind group to share progress, brainstorm solutions, and hold each other accountable for commitments.
  • Offer value to potential Master Mind partners by helping them with their goals, creating mutually beneficial relationships built on reciprocity.
  • Study and learn from existing successful Master Mind groups in your field, modeling their structures and practices for your own alliance.
10
The Mystery of Sex Transmutation
The emotion of sex is the most powerful of all human emotions, and when this powerful driving force is redirected and channeled into creative efforts, it becomes a tremendous source of energy for achievement and success. This principle teaches that sexual energy is creative energy that can be transmuted into other forms of creative expression, including artistic endeavors, business innovations, and the accumulation of wealth through sustained, passionate effort. The transmutation of sex energy requires self-discipline and the conscious redirection of this powerful force toward the achievement of your definite major purpose, creating an intensity of desire and focus that dramatically accelerates your progress. History's most successful individuals have learned to harness this creative energy, using it to fuel their persistence, enhance their imagination, and maintain the burning desire necessary for extraordinary achievement. This transmutation process not only conserves vital energy but also develops personal magnetism, confidence, and creative ability that attracts success, opportunities, and the cooperation of others toward the fulfillment of your goals.
Daily Application Exercises
  • Channel your creative energy into productive activities related to your goals whenever you feel the urge for immediate gratification or distraction.
  • Practice self-discipline in all areas of life to strengthen your ability to direct your energies toward your most important objectives rather than temporary pleasures.
  • Engage in physical exercise and creative activities that allow you to express and redirect your vital energy into constructive channels that support your success.
  • Develop inspiring relationships with people who elevate your thinking and encourage your highest aspirations, surrounding yourself with positive influences that support your growth.

"Whatever the mind can conceive and believe, it can achieve." - Napoleon Hill
